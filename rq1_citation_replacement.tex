We separate three sources of long horizon performance with three sub-studies.
\textbf{E1} fixes the harness at Claude Code and varies the backbone across eight flagship models, isolating model capability under a strong fixed scaffold~\citep{anthropic_opus47,openai_gpt55,zhipu_glm51,deepseek_v4_pro,google_gemini31_pro,qwen36_plus,moonshot_kimi_k26,xai_grok41_fast_reasoning}.
\textbf{E2} fixes the backbone at \texttt{gpt-5.4}~\citep{openai_gpt54} and varies the harness across six families spanning lightweight shell loops, terminal agents with persistent memory, and modular multi-tool frameworks, isolating scaffold quality under a fixed mid-tier backbone (per-agent wrappers and the credential / workspace contract are detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:adaptations}).
\textbf{E3} traces within-family scaling for the GPT, Claude Opus, and Qwen lineages, each paired with its provider-native agent so intra-family deltas reflect generational capability rather than scaffolding fit~\citep{openai_gpt4o_system_card,openai_gpt5,openai_gpt52_system_card,openai_gpt55,anthropic_claude4_system_card,anthropic_sonnet45,anthropic_opus45,anthropic_opus47,qwen25_technical_report,qwen3max,qwen35_plus,qwen36_plus}.
